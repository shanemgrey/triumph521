# Chapter 05 Spec — Rent: Income from Exclusion

## Chapter
- Number: 05
- Working title: Rent: Income from Exclusion
- Target length: ~2,500 words (variance allowed)

## Chapter Job (non-overlapping)
Define **rent** precisely and separate it from wages and profit using simple contrasts (land vs improvements; scarcity vs production).

## Reader Entry → Exit State
- Entry: The reader understands exclusion but may still conflate rent with profit or “success”.
- Exit: The reader can spot rent extraction and sees why it scales with civilization and scarcity.

## Must Include
- Clear definitions: wages/profit vs rent.
- The land/improvements split: same building, different location price.
- The resource parallel: scarcity rents vs productive returns.

## Must Not Include
- No moral blame (“greed”) as explanation.
- No deep policy architecture yet.

## Key Claims (IDs)
- `C-002`: rent differs structurally from productive income.
- `C-003`: location premium is socially generated.

## Evidence Plan
- Thought experiments + concrete contrasts; avoid empirical claims unless necessary.

## Glossary / Terms
- Introduce in this chapter: Rent (economic rent).
- Safe to use: Exclusion, Commons.

## Section Plan (Outline)
1. Why “profit vs rent” confusion blocks solutions
2. The improvements/land split (intuition)
3. Rent scales with civilization and scarcity
4. Rent extraction acts like a hidden tax on everyone else (setup)

## Compression Target (5 bullets)
- Rent is income from exclusive control of a scarce input.
- Wages and profit come from producing goods/services.
- Location premium is civilization value, not the owner’s labor.
- Rent grows as civilization and scarcity grow.
- Next: rent capture warps politics and policy.
